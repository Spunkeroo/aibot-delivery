15 Ways to Save Money on Delivery Apps in 2026

By aibot.delivery | March 1, 2026 | 8 min read

Subscription Stacking

1. Get DashPass ($9.99/mo) — pays for itself after 2 orders with free delivery on $12+ orders. 2. Use Uber One ($9.99/mo) — covers rides AND food delivery. 3. Try Walmart+ ($12.95/mo) — free grocery delivery with no price markup. 4. Amazon Prime includes free Amazon Fresh delivery on $50+ orders. 5. Don't stack all at once — rotate: use DashPass for 3 months, then switch to Uber One for 3 months to get new-subscriber deals on each.

Promo Code Strategies

6. Check aibot.coupons before every order — AI-verified promo codes. 7. Create a new account with a different email for first-time user discounts (most apps offer $10-20 off first 3 orders). 8. Sign up for app notifications — DoorDash and Uber Eats send personalized promo codes weekly. 9. Follow delivery apps on social media — they often post exclusive codes during slow periods. 10. Check your email spam folder — win-back offers with big discounts often land there.

Smart Ordering Habits

11. Use pickup instead of delivery when the restaurant is within 10 minutes — save $5-10 in fees per order. 12. Order during off-peak times (2-5pm or after 8pm) — no surge pricing and faster delivery. 13. Increase your order size — the percentage-based service fee stays the same, so one $40 order costs less in fees than two $20 orders. 14. Compare prices across apps — the same meal can vary $5-10 between DoorDash, Uber Eats, and Grubhub.

Credit Card & Cashback Hacks

15. Use a rewards credit card for delivery purchases. Cards like Chase Sapphire Reserve earn 3x points on dining (including delivery). American Express Gold earns 4x on restaurants. Capital One SavorOne earns 3% cashback on dining. Stack this with cashback portals: Rakuten sometimes offers 3-5% back on delivery apps. On a $30 order, you could earn $1-3 back just from your card plus cashback — that adds up to $500+/year for regular users.

When to Skip Delivery Entirely

Sometimes the best savings hack is not ordering delivery. Cook batch meals on Sundays — costs $3-5/meal versus $20-30 delivered. Keep a list of quick 15-minute meals for when you're tempted to order. Use aibot.delivery to compare: if delivery fees + markups exceed 30% of the food cost, it's almost always cheaper to pick up or cook. The money you save compounds — $15/order saved × 3 orders/week = $2,340/year back in your pocket.
