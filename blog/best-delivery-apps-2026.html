Best Delivery Apps Compared: DoorDash vs Uber Eats vs Instacart in 2026

By aibot.delivery | March 1, 2026 | 8 min read

DoorDash

DoorDash is the market leader with 67% of US food delivery market share. Pros: Largest restaurant selection, DashPass ($9.99/mo) eliminates delivery fees on orders over $12, excellent pickup option that avoids all fees. Cons: Service fees (10-15%) are higher than competitors, prices are often marked up 15-20% versus in-store. Best for: Restaurant variety and suburban areas where DoorDash has the most driver coverage.

Uber Eats

Uber Eats is the #2 platform globally with the broadest international coverage. Pros: Uber One membership ($9.99/mo) covers both rides and deliveries, often has the most promo codes, fastest delivery in urban areas. Cons: Surge pricing during peak hours, smaller restaurant selection than DoorDash in suburban areas. Best for: Urban dwellers and existing Uber users who get bundled savings.

Instacart

Instacart dominates grocery delivery with partnerships at 1,400+ retail banners. Pros: Same-day grocery delivery, Instacart+ ($9.99/mo) includes free delivery on $35+ orders, comparison shopping across stores. Cons: Item prices marked up 5-15% vs in-store, replacement choices can miss the mark. Best for: Grocery shopping when you can't get to the store, especially for bulk orders where the time savings justify the markup.

Other Apps Worth Considering

Grubhub — often has the best restaurant-exclusive deals and free food promotions. Gopuff — instant delivery of convenience items from their own warehouses (no markup). Amazon Fresh — free delivery for Prime members on $50+ orders with competitive grocery prices. Walmart+ ($12.95/mo) — free delivery from Walmart stores with no markup on items. Shipt — Target-owned grocery delivery with same-day service.

How to Save on Every Delivery

1) Compare prices across apps before ordering — the same restaurant often has different prices. 2) Use pickup when possible to avoid delivery fees entirely. 3) Order during off-peak hours (2-5pm) to avoid surge pricing. 4) Stack subscriptions strategically — DashPass for food, Instacart+ for groceries. 5) Check aibot.coupons for active promo codes before every order. 6) Tip in cash — some apps reduce driver base pay when you tip in-app.
